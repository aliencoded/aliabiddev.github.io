Wizard Route — Support

Need help? Read on, or email aliabidx@gmail.com.

How to play

  • Goal: survive four themed levels (forest, desert, underground, volcano) and defeat the boss at the end of each.
  • Movement: drag horizontally on screen to steer your wizard squad left and right.
  • Speed: tap and hold the WALK or RUN button at the bottom corners to slow down or sprint.
  • Combat: your wizards auto-fire at any enemies in range — different classes have different attack patterns.
  • Gates: shoot red brick walls to break them, run through chests for upgrades, free wizard statues to unlock new classes. Avoid red holes in the floor.
  • Boss fight: when you reach the boss, the screen transitions to a circular arena. Drag to orbit around the boss while your wizards keep firing. Dodge slams, projectile volleys, charges, and homing missiles.
  • Endless mode: after clearing all four levels, you can continue forever with increasing difficulty.

Health and lives

  • HP (♥) — how much damage you can take before going down. Starts at 3.
  • Lives (◆) — per run. When your HP hits 0 you lose a life and respawn at a checkpoint with full HP. Lose all 3 lives and the run is over — back to the start.
  • Skeletons sometimes drop coins, health packs (refill HP), or shields (block all damage for 100m).

Tips

  • Brick walls cost 1 HP if you run through them unbroken — destroy them at range.
  • Holes in the floor are an instant life loss — watch the path carefully.
  • Don't shoot civilians. Goats and villagers occasionally cross the path; hitting one with a projectile costs you a whole life.
  • During the boss's cyan shield phase, the boss takes no damage but attacks faster — focus on dodging.
  • Practice Mode in Settings makes you take no damage if you want to learn the boss patterns.

Desktop controls

If you're running on macOS or in Unity Editor:

  • ← → or A / D — steer
  • ↑ or W — sprint
  • ↓ or S — walk
  • Mouse drag — also steers (hold and drag)

Common issues

  • Game feels slow or stutters: try restarting the app. The game uses procedural generation at startup and benefits from a clean launch.
  • Music sounds repetitive: open Settings → Music and pick a different track, or set it to OFF.
  • No vibration: ensure your iPhone's haptics are enabled in iOS Settings → Sounds & Haptics. The in-game Vibration toggle must also be on.
